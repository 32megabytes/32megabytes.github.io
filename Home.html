ABOUT / Faq

What is Finding Subtext?

Finding Subtext is a passion project of mine. Established in 2019, Finding Subtext aims to examine and analyze content; usually within political, social, or conspiratorial fringe communities. The show comes in two forms, "Mini-Episodes" which are short small-topic videos, and "Episodes" which aim to hyper-analyze a given topic.

Who are you?

My "name" is Seth Smyth. I go by random names online to protect my privacy. I'm a queer 20-something with way too much going on. I've worked many jobs over the years; professional photography, cinematography, video editing, and I'm currently pursuing an education in clinical psychology. I aim to bring all these skills to Finding Subtext, along with my own personal experiences. I've also been on YouTube since 2007 making videos on whatever interests me at the time. I've created channels with 40+ thousand subscribers, and numerous tiny channels nobody remembers; what matters to me is that I'm making something I can be proud of at the time.

Can we collab?

Yes! I love collaborating with other creators. Contact me at my business email or via DM on my socials.

What's your upload schedule?

I release episodes as quickly as I can, which isn't always very fast. Once I reach my donation goals I'll be able to work on my channel full time, but that's a lofty goal for the time being.

What equipment do you use?

Currently I'm filming most of my A-Roll on a green screen using a Sony A7iv with 35mm 1.8 lens. For other scenes it really varies.
